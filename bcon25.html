CORE DEVELOPMENT

5 talks
Slot It Like It's Hot: Progress on the New Animation System
Blender Animation & Rigging Dev Team
ANIMATION DEVELOPMENT BAKLAVA
▶ WATCH
Progress update on Project Baklava — Blender's new layered animation system that replaces the legacy Action data-block. The new system allows a single Animation data-block to control multiple objects simultaneously and introduces true animation layers. This is one of the most significant architectural changes to Blender's animation system in years.
Why it matters: Layered animation means NLA-style blending, multi-character animation, and proper shot-level animation management — features the industry has needed for years.
Rigging With Nodes Changes Everything!
Blender Rigging Developers
RIGGING NODES DEVELOPMENT
▶ WATCH
Introduction to Rig Nodes — a node-based approach to building rigs in Blender that promises to revolutionize how rigs are authored. Instead of Python scripts and complex driver networks, riggers will be able to build procedural, reusable rig components with visual node graphs. Covers the motivation, prototype state, and future direction.
Timeline: Still in prototype/development at time of BCON25 — this is a sneak peek at Blender's rigging future. Watch this alongside the "Slot It" talk for the full animation overhaul picture.
Extensions Are Coming: Rethinking Add-on Development
Jan-Hendrik Müller, Brady Johnston
SCRIPTING EXTENSIONS PYTHON
▶ WATCH
The new Blender Extensions platform (extensions.blender.org) replaces the old add-on system. This talk explains what changes for developers: package management, dependencies, Python environments, and the new submission/discovery workflow. Unlocks the full potential of Python's ecosystem (pip packages, external libs) within Blender extensions.
External references
extensions.blender.org · Python packaging (PyPI) · Virtual environments in Blender
import bpy: Modern Add-on Development
Brady Johnston
SCRIPTING PYTHON DEVELOPMENT
▶ WATCH
A practical deep-dive into the best practices for writing modern Blender add-ons in 2025. Covers type annotations, proper use of the bpy API, testing strategies, and how to take advantage of the Extensions system. Companion to the "Extensions are Coming" talk.
Improving Cycles CPU Performance with Clang & Hardware Profiled Optimization
Blender Cycles Developer
CYCLES PERFORMANCE CPU
▶ WATCH
Technical deep-dive into how Cycles' CPU performance is being improved using hardware-profiled optimizations and the Clang compiler. Explains why render times vary so dramatically across CPU architectures and what the team is doing about it — including PGO (Profile-Guided Optimization) and architecture-specific builds.
Practical impact: Faster CPU renders for everyone — especially relevant for users without a GPU or who use CPU for final renders.

RENDERING & COMPOSITING

6 talks
Compositing 5.0: Powerful yet Simple
Habib Gahbiche — Blender Compositor Developer
COMPOSITING VFX ROADMAP
▶ WATCH
Showcase of the massive improvements coming to Blender's compositor — many already shipping in Blender 4.x. New nodes, GPU acceleration, real-time compositing in the viewport, and a redesigned node system aimed at both power VFX users and casual compositors. Habib also shares the roadmap and explains how to contribute feedback through the community.
Key features
GPU-accelerated compositing · New node types · Improved color management · Roadmap preview · Community contribution pathway
One of the top 10 favorite talks at BCON25. Must-watch for anyone doing post-production in Blender.
The Future of Rendering
Jules Urbach (CEO, OTOY / Render Network)
RENDERING AI GPU INDUSTRY
▶ WATCH
Jules Urbach (creator of Octane Renderer, RNDR Network) presents his vision for rendering into the 2030s: GPU path tracing evolving with AI denoising and neural rendering, real-time ray tracing closing the gap with offline, and distributed cloud rendering via decentralized networks. Covers implications for gaming, VFX, media, and art.
External references
OTOY / OctaneRender · RNDR Network (decentralized GPU rendering) · Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) · AI denoising
Bringing Real Cameras to Cycles
Blender Cycles Developer
CYCLES CAMERA REALISM
▶ WATCH
Work on importing real-world camera lens data into Cycles for physically accurate rendering. Covers lens distortion, chromatic aberration, vignetting, and bokeh shape — simulated from actual lens profiles rather than approximations. Huge for archviz, VFX integration, and photorealistic work where matching real camera looks is essential.
Light My Shiny Render Pass!
Yaraslau Lebedzeu
RENDERING PHYSICS CYCLES
▶ WATCH
An optics deep-dive: why 3D renders don't always look real. Explores physical phenomena that current RGB rendering approximates poorly — birefringence (materials that split light by polarization), gradient index of refraction, diffraction, and spectral rendering. Discusses the limits of what Cycles can accurately simulate and where shortcuts are hiding.
Best for: Anyone doing product visualization or photo-real renders wanting to understand why "something feels off" even in technically correct renders.
Path Tracing Through Acrylic: A Photorealism Journey on Render Network
Artist / Render Network
CYCLES PHOTOREALISM CLOUD RENDERING
▶ WATCH
Artist journey into hyper-realistic acrylic/resin material rendering using path tracing. Discusses material setup, subsurface scattering, internal caustics, and using the RNDR cloud rendering network to handle the heavy compute loads.
The Illusion Engine: The Secrets to Realism
TBA
PHOTOREALISM SHADING
▶ WATCH
Explores the artistic and technical decisions that push renders from "good CG" to "indistinguishable from photo." Covers imperfections, micro-detail, lighting subtlety, and how human perception fills in detail — and how to exploit that. A practical guide to photorealism philosophy.

GEOMETRY NODES

7 talks
Custom Particle Systems in Geometry Nodes
Kris Meeusen
GEO NODES SIMULATION VFX
▶ WATCH
Shows how to build fully custom particle systems entirely inside Geometry Nodes — bypassing the legacy particle system. With the Simulation Zones introduced in Blender 4.1, artists can create physically-motivated effects (flocking, gravity, collisions, life cycles) entirely through nodes, with complete artist control. Covers practical examples and node graph architecture strategies.
Hobbyist tip: The legacy particle system is being sunset — this is the path forward for all effects work.
Making a Music Video in Geometry Nodes for OK Go
Motion Designer / OK Go Collaborator
GEO NODES MOTION DESIGN MUSIC VIDEO
▶ WATCH
A behind-the-scenes look at using Geometry Nodes to create generative, data-driven visuals for a music video for the band OK Go (known for their elaborate, one-take videos). Covers procedural choreography, audio-reactive geometry, and using Geo Nodes as an expressive motion-design tool rather than just a modelling system.
One of the conference's crowd favorites. Shows Geo Nodes as a creative medium, not just a technical one.
Making a Spectrogram with Geometry Nodes
Community Developer
GEO NODES DATA VIZ AUDIO
▶ WATCH
A clever project using Geometry Nodes to build an audio spectrogram — a visual frequency analysis of sound — directly in Blender. Demonstrates the power of Blender as a data-visualization tool and pushes the creative boundaries of what the node system can do. Great crossover between audio production and 3D.
From Pen Plotters to Robots: Geometry Nodes for Parametric Toolpathing
Maker / Hardware Artist
GEO NODES ROBOTICS FABRICATION
▶ WATCH
Using Geometry Nodes to generate machine toolpaths — G-code sequences for CNC machines, pen plotters, and robot arms — entirely procedurally. Shows Blender as a parametric design-to-fabrication tool. An unusual and inspiring use case that expands how artists and engineers think about the software.
Creating the Cutest Stylized Water with Geometry Nodes
Procedural Artist
GEO NODES STYLIZED SHADING
▶ WATCH
How to create appealing, toon/stylized water simulations using Geometry Nodes rather than physics simulations. Procedural wave generation, foam lines, ripples — all controllable and art-directable. Ideal for animation and game asset pipelines where physical accuracy is less important than visual charm.
Squishy Volumes at Your Fingertips
Blender Community Developer
GEO NODES VOLUMES SIMULATION
▶ WATCH
Procedural squishy/jelly volume effects using Geometry Nodes and/or the volume pipeline. Explores soft-body-like behavior without full simulation — great for game-ready assets and animation effects. Covers the node architecture behind secondary motion and squash-and-stretch in volumes.
Bridging Grease Pencil and Geometry Nodes — 2.5D Illustration, Lighting, Animation and Compositing Workflows
Research / Diploma Project
GEO NODES GREASE PENCIL 2.5D
▶ WATCH
A diploma research project revealing a node-based toolset (targeted prototype release mid-2026) for sophisticated 2.5D animation. Bridges Grease Pencil strokes with Geometry Nodes to enable: real-time relighting of meshes and GP strokes, procedural stroke/fill generators on live render passes, clipping solvers for 2D layers in 3D space, and directable level-of-detail blending. Essentially building a full 2.5D animation framework inside Blender.
Prototype release: Expected mid-2026. One of the most technically ambitious research talks of the conference.

ClayPencil is a workflow/add-on that enables rigging-free 3D character animation — closer to traditional frame-by-frame 2D animation but living in a 3D space. Keymesh is a free add-on enabling per-frame mesh replacement — letting Blender mimic the "boiling" style of hand-drawn frame-by-frame animation with 3D geometry. The talk covers the full spectrum of what Keymesh enables: squash-and-stretch on meshes, morphing effects, non-destructive shape exploration, and integration into a full animation pipeline.

GAME DEVELOPMENT

5 talks
Blue Prince: Beyond the Doors
Davide Pellino — Art Director, Dogubomb
GAME ART STYLIZED 1 SHADER
▶ WATCH
Blue Prince launched April 2025 as the highest-rated game of the year on Metacritic (93/100). Its entire visual world — every room, environment, and asset — was created in Blender using just one shader and four textures. Davide Pellino reveals the elegant, constraint-driven approach behind the game's iconic look and how restricting the toolset actually unlocked creativity.
One of the top 10 favorites at BCON25. Inspiring for game devs and artists who feel the need for complex setups — this talk proves less really is more.

It enables a single artist or small team to take a character from design through to final render. Covers their universal character creator, procedural environments, and the R&D philosophy of empowering artists to do more with less.

The Optimization Iceberg — Making 3D Run on Any Device
Technical Artist
GAME ART OPTIMIZATION PERFORMANCE
▶ WATCH
Most 3D optimization is discussed at the tip-of-the-iceberg level (reduce polys, bake textures). This talk goes deep: draw calls, shader complexity, texture streaming, occlusion culling, LOD systems, and the non-obvious performance traps that kill performance on mobile and low-spec devices. Relevant for both game devs and anyone deploying 3D to the web.
