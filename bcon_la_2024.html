Historic event: BCON LA was the first-ever official US Blender Conference. Organized by CG Cookie and Autotroph (Jonathan Williamson's company), in partnership with the Blender Foundation. Ton Roosendaal attended but did not organize — he was able to enjoy the conference as a guest for once. All recordings are on the CG Cookie YouTube channel. BCON North America returns in Austin, Texas, April 13–14 2026.

DAY 1 — FRIDAY, APRIL 19

MAIN STAGE + SIDE BAR TRACKS
Keynote
Jonathan Williamson — Autotroph / CG Cookie
KEYNOTECOMMUNITY
▶ PLAYLIST
The opening keynote for the first US Blender Conference. Jonathan Williamson, as organizer, welcomes attendees and sets the stage. Ton Roosendaal was present and spoke briefly — notably happy to enjoy a BCON without having to run it.
Barnstorm VFX: Leveraging Blender in the Industry
Christopher Parks — Barnstorm VFX
VFXINDUSTRYPIPELINE
▶ PLAYLIST
How Barnstorm VFX, a professional US VFX studio, integrates Blender into commercial and broadcast production pipelines. Covers specific projects, the business case for Blender adoption in a studio context, and workflow integration with other tools.
Industry signal: A US studio talking openly about professional Blender usage at an in-person conference — a milestone for Blender's professional credibility in North America.
An Accidental Animation Studio: CG Cookie Collabs
Kent Trammell — CG Cookie
ANIMATIONCOMMUNITYINDIE
▶ PLAYLIST
How CG Cookie's collaborative animation projects grew from tutorial content into something closer to a real studio. Kent shares the creative process, team dynamics of remote collaboration, and how learner projects evolved into polished short films.
Dark Ages — Production Techniques
Andrew Weidenhammer
ANIMATIONSTYLIZEDPRODUCTION
▶ PLAYLIST
Behind the scenes on "Dark Ages," an animated project with a distinctive medieval/fantasy aesthetic built in Blender. Covers stylized rendering, character design, and production techniques for a solo or small team working on an ambitious project.
Remixing Real Life
Ian Hubert
VFXPHOTOGRAMMETRYWORLD BUILDING
▶ WATCH
Ian Hubert — famous for his "Lazy Tutorials" series and cinematic Blender VFX work — shares his photoscan workflow and world-building philosophy. He demonstrates how to use photogrammetry to capture real-world textures and geometry, then remix them into cinematic 3D environments. This was one of the most anticipated talks of the event.
Ian Hubert is known for achieving jaw-dropping cinematic results with a minimalist workflow — this talk is essential viewing for anyone interested in photorealistic compositing or indie filmmaking with Blender.
Magically VFXlicious — A Peek Behind Zach King's Viral Videos
KiJeon Nam
VFXSOCIAL MEDIATRICKS
▶ PLAYLIST
An inside look at the VFX pipeline behind Zach King's viral magic trick videos (billions of views on TikTok/YouTube). Covers the techniques used to seamlessly blend practical shots with Blender VFX for tight social media turnarounds. A rare look at high-volume, high-quality short-form VFX production.
Weaving Code and Art Together
Spencer Magnusson
SCRIPTINGADDONSGENERATIVE
▶ PLAYLIST
How to combine Python scripting with artistic intent — generative art, procedural tools, and creative coding in Blender. Spencer is known for his add-on development work and accessible coding education for artists.
2D/3D Hybrid Storyboarding with VISION
Maciej Gliwa
STORYBOARD2D/3DPREVIS
▶ PLAYLIST
Introduction to VISION — a workflow/tool for creating hybrid 2D/3D storyboards in Blender. Designed for filmmakers and animators who want the flexibility of drawn storyboards with the spatial awareness of 3D layouts.
Reinventing Virtual Production with Jetset
Eliot Mack
VIRTUAL PRODUCTIONMOBILEFILM
▶ PLAYLIST
Jetset is a mobile app for iPhone/iPad that turns your phone into a live camera tracker for virtual production. The talk covers how Jetset integrates with Blender for on-set visualization, LED volume work, and previs — dramatically reducing the cost barrier for virtual production.
References
Jetset app (iOS) · iPhone LiDAR tracking · Virtual production pipeline · LED volume workflows
Pataz Animation: A Different Way of Working Together
Daniel Salazar — Pataz Animation
ANIMATIONSTUDIOPIPELINE
▶ PLAYLIST
How Pataz Animation Studio structures its team and workflow differently from conventional studios. Daniel Salazar (known in the Blender community as "ZanQdo") shares lessons from building a distributed, Blender-first animation team.
Beyond Business as Usual
Francesco Siddi, Kyle Unzicker
BUSINESSBLENDER FOUNDATIONCOMMUNITY
▶ PLAYLIST
Francesco Siddi (Blender Foundation) and Kyle Unzicker discuss the business and organizational model behind Blender's sustainable open-source development — how the foundation works, how it's funded, and what "beyond business as usual" means for free software in the creative industry.
A History of Crazy Uses for Our Favorite Program
Jason van Gumster
COMMUNITYHISTORYFUN
▶ PLAYLIST
Jason van Gumster (author of "Blender For Dummies") takes a humorous look at the most unexpected, creative, and downright bizarre uses of Blender throughout its history — from scientific visualization to stage performance to Blender-made NASA presentations.
What is NPR? An Exploration of Why Stylization is Important — Panel
Dillon Gu (Dillongoo Studios)
NPRSTYLIZEDPANEL
▶ PLAYLIST
A panel discussion exploring Non-Photorealistic Rendering (NPR) — why stylization matters as an artistic and storytelling choice, not just a technical shortcut. Dillon Gu is the creator of Dillongoo Studios, known for expressive stylized animation and tutorials.
I Really Wanted a Life-Sized T. Rex Skull
Alex Chamberlain
3D PRINTINGPALEONTOLOGYPASSION PROJECT
▶ PLAYLIST
Alex Chamberlain's journey to create a life-sized, 3D-printed Tyrannosaurus Rex skull using Blender and publicly available fossil scan data. Covers the modeling process, scale challenges, 3D printing workflow, and the community aspect of open paleontology data.
Also appeared at BCON25 as a lightning talk. An inspiring example of Blender for personal passion projects with real-world physical output.
The Future of Addons and Assets in Blender Market and Beyond
Amber Kelly — Blender Market / Superhive
MARKETPLACEECOSYSTEMBUSINESS
▶ PLAYLIST
The Blender Market (now rebranded to Superhive) discusses its future roadmap: supporting the new Extensions platform, how the marketplace will evolve for Blender 4.x add-ons, and the business model for independent developers selling Blender tools.
Important announcement at this event: Blender Market rebranded to Superhive — a significant change for the ecosystem.
Storyboarding in 3D Space
Paul Caggegi
STORYBOARDPREVISANIMATION
▶ PLAYLIST
How to use Blender's 3D environment for storyboarding — a workflow that gives artists camera angle, lighting, and spatial relationship information that 2D storyboards can't provide. Paul also gave a Grease Pencil life drawing tutorial in the Side Bar track.
Tiki — A Community Feature Film
Robert Rioux
FILMCOMMUNITYFEATURE
▶ PLAYLIST
Robert Rioux shares the development of Tiki — an ambitious community-driven animated feature film project built entirely in Blender by a distributed volunteer team. Covers the production model, challenges of coordinating a large volunteer cast, and the current status of the project.
The Future of Rendering
Jules Urbach — OTOY / Render Network
RENDERINGAIGPU
▶ PLAYLIST
Jules Urbach (CEO of OTOY, creator of Octane Renderer) presents his vision for GPU and AI-driven rendering into the 2030s. He gave a similar version of this talk at BCON25, but the BCON LA edition was the first time he presented it. Covers real-time ray tracing, neural rendering, and decentralized cloud rendering via the RNDR network.
References
OTOY / OctaneRender · RNDR Network · AI denoising · Neural radiance fields · GPU path tracing
Previs & Postvis: Elevate Your Filmmaking
Colin Levy
FILMPREVISVFX
▶ PLAYLIST
Colin Levy — director of Blender's open movie "Tears of Steel" and filmmaker — explains how to use Blender for pre-visualization (previs) and post-visualization (postvis) in film production. How 3D previsualization saves time, money, and creative energy on set.
Colin Levy is known for his Emmy Award-winning VFX work on "Lost" and as director of Blender's "Tears of Steel" open movie.
The Quest for the Next Step — Making Of "Freelance"
Luciano A. Muñoz Sessarego
SHORT FILMINDIEANIMATION
▶ PLAYLIST
Making of "Freelance" — an animated short film. Covers the creative and technical challenges of pushing personal work to the next level, managing scope, and developing a distinctive visual style in Blender.
3D Paper Craft with a Cutting Plotter Machine
Xury Greer
FABRICATIONCRAFTPAPER
▶ PLAYLIST
Using Blender to design paper craft models exported for cutting plotter machines (like a Cricut or Silhouette). Covers the unfolding workflow (converting 3D models to flat net patterns), designing for cut-and-fold, and building physical paper sculptures from Blender designs.
SIDE BAR WORKSHOPS — DAY 1
Grease Pencil Life Drawing Demonstration Workshop
Paul Caggegi
GREASE PENCILDRAWING
A live life drawing session using Blender's Grease Pencil — demonstrating how traditional figure drawing translates to digital 3D sketching in a 3D space.
Procedural Shading for Automotive Visualization Workshop
Odilon Loiez
AUTOMOTIVESHADINGPROCEDURAL
A hands-on workshop building car paint and automotive material shaders procedurally in Blender. Covers metallic flake, clearcoat, and material variation for product visualization workflows.
Ten Thousand Swords: A Geometry Nodes Vertical Slice Workshop
Oscar Baechler
GEO NODESPROCEDURAL
A deep vertical slice into Geometry Nodes — building a system capable of generating thousands of unique swords procedurally. Covers node architecture, instancing, variation systems, and performance considerations.
Everything Can Loop! My Workflow for Looping Animations Workshop
Nathan Duck
ANIMATIONLOOPINGWORKFLOW
How to design and execute seamless looping animations in Blender — from simple procedural loops to complex character animation cycles. Covers graph editor techniques, NLA editor, and the mindset of designing for loops from the start.
Texturing Techniques for Starship Interiors and Exteriors Workshop
Stephen Burns
TEXTURINGSCI-FIPROPS
Practical texturing workflows for hard-surface sci-fi assets — specifically starship interiors and exteriors. Covers material layering, greeble paneling shaders, wear and tear, and lighting-responsive materials for cinematic space environments.
Demystifying Add-ons: A Workshop Workshop
Spencer Magnusson
SCRIPTINGADDONSPYTHON
A beginner-to-intermediate hands-on workshop in writing Blender add-ons with Python. Covers the bpy API, operator creation, panels, and how to go from "I want to automate this" to a working add-on that others can use.
Retargeting and Mocap for Animators Workshop
Brad Clark
ANIMATIONMOCAPRIGGING
How to bring motion capture data into Blender and retarget it to custom rigs. Covers clean-up workflows, common mocap artifacts, and how to blend mocap with hand-keyed animation to retain artistic control.
Sculpt Like You're Drawing Workshop
Oscar Baechler
SCULPTINGWORKFLOW
A workflow approach to Blender sculpting that treats the 3D surface like a drawing canvas — loose, gestural, intuitive. Aimed at 2D artists moving into sculpting who find traditional polygon workflows too technical.

MOTION GRAPHICSCORPORATEPIPELINE
▶ PLAYLIST

Dr. Jon Denning

▶ PLAYLIST
A decade retrospective on RetopoFlow — one of Blender's most successful and widely used retopology add-ons. Dr. Jon Denning covers the technical and business journey of building a professional Blender tool, lessons learned from 10 years of user feedback, and where the tool is going next.

RetopoFlow add-on (CG Cookie) · Retopology workflows · Blender add-on business model
Blending Subjects for Real-World Education

How Blender is being integrated into school curricula to teach STEM and creative subjects simultaneously — using 3D modeling and animation as a bridge between mathematics, science, design, and storytelling for K-12 students.
Blending 3D VFX and 2D Drawings in the Making of "A Light in the Woods"
Stef Corazza
VFX2D/3DANIMATION
▶ PLAYLIST
Making of "A Light in the Woods" — a hybrid animated/drawn short that combines traditional 2D hand-drawn frames with Blender 3D environments and VFX. Covers the pipeline for mixing drawing and 3D, compositing approaches, and the creative rationale for the hybrid technique.
Photorealistic NPR: Replicating 90's Cel Animation
Chris Boylan
NPRANIME90s STYLE
▶ PLAYLIST
How to achieve the distinctive look of 90s hand-drawn cel animation (think classic anime like Sailor Moon, Dragon Ball Z, or early Miyazaki) using Blender's NPR shading tools. How traditional artists (illustrators, painters, comic artists) can use Blender as a previsualization tool — setting up 3D reference scenes, lighting rigs, and camera angles to inform their 2D work. Makes Blender a "digital maquette" tool rather than a full 3D production environment.

Adam Preisler

Building rich surrounding environments for architectural visualization — beyond just the building itself. Covers landscaping, vegetation, people, vehicles, and atmospheric context that make architectural renders feel lived-in and believable.
